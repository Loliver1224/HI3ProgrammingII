World's First 3D Printed Heart Made Using Patinent's Own Cells

Researchers at Tel Avin University in Israel say they have made the world's first 3D
printed heart with cells and blood vessels. Hearts have been 3D printed in the past, but
onlt using simple tissues without cells or blood vessels.

"Our 3D heart is small - the size of a rabbit's heart," says Tel Avin University's Professor
Tal Dvir. "But larger human hearts require the same technology."

The heart was made using cells taken from a living person. Making a heart from the cells
of the person who receive it should remove the risk of the body rejecting it, Dvir
explains.

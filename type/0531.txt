Japanese University to Stop Hiring Professors Who Smoke

Nagasaki University says not hire professors who smoke, unless they quit smoking
before they start working there, saying that smokers are not suitable for work in the
education industry.

The decision makes Nagasaki University the first public university to have a rule against
hiring smokers. The university will also ban all tabacco products and items connected
with smoking from its campus starting in April 2020. It promises to help both teachers
and students who want to stop smoking.
